PELS keeps your home under a chosen power limit and moves EV charging, heating, hot water, or ventilation to the cheapest hours — automatically lowering, pausing, or turning off lower-priority devices when needed. Or set a goal with a deadline, like the car charged by morning, and PELS finds the cheapest way to hit it. It watches whole-home power from Homey Energy, Tibber Pulse, AMS/HAN/P1 meters, or Flow data, and acts before the limit is crossed; when available power returns, it resumes devices in priority order.

PELS (Pris- og Effektstyrt Energilagringssystem) can use spot prices from Homey Energy or external Flow prices; in Norway it can also factor in grid tariffs and strømstøtte/Norgespris. Flexible devices run in cheaper hours when there is room, while the power limit still takes priority. Use modes such as Home, Away and Night to give each routine its own comfort targets and priorities, and set a daily budget when you want PELS to pace energy use across the day. One-off goals with a deadline — charging the EV by morning, warming a room before you get home, or heating water before it is needed — are called Smart tasks in the app.
